# Hackathon Presentation Guideline

You've built something. Now show it. This is what your presentation should cover.

---

## 1. The Problem (1–2 min)

- What real problem are you solving?
- Who experiences this problem?
- Why does it matter — in Tanzania, in Africa?

Be specific. "Farmers struggle with delayed payments" is better than "the world needs blockchain."

## 2. Your Solution (2–3 min)

- What did you build?
- How does it work — show the user flow step by step.
- Who is the user, and what do they do?

Demo the main action. Start the app, connect wallet, do the thing.

## 3. Why Blockchain? Why Avalanche? (1 min)

- Where does blockchain actually add value in your solution?
- Why Avalanche specifically — speed, cost, Avalanche L1s, ecosystem?

Do not force blockchain in where it doesn't help. Honest answers are better than fake ones.

## 4. Technical Highlights (1–2 min)

- Key smart contract function or feature you built.
- One interesting challenge you solved.
- What works, what is still rough.

You do not need to explain every line. Show the meaningful part.

## 5. Demo (2–3 min)

This is the most important section. Show it working.

- **Live demo preferred** — run the app or testnet interaction.
- **Failing gracefully is fine** — if it breaks, explain what it should do and why it matters.
- **Fallback: screenshots or video** if live is risky.

Tips:
- Open wallets and accounts before the demo starts.
- Have test data ready (sample certificates, sample transactions, etc.).
- Talk through what is happening on screen.

## 6. Impact & Next Steps (1 min)

- Who would actually use this?
- What would you build next with more time?
- Could this become a real product, campus tool, or community solution?

---

## Slide Structure (Suggested — 6–8 slides)

| Slide | What to cover |
|---|---|
| 1. Title | Project name, team name, track, members |
| 2. Problem | The real problem, who faces it, why it matters |
| 3. Solution | Overview, user flow, key features |
| 4. Why Blockchain / Avalanche | Genuine value, not buzzwords |
| 5. Technical Overview | Architecture, contract(s), stack |
| 6. Demo | Screenshots or live walkthrough |
| 7. Impact & Roadmap | Users, next steps, potential |
| 8. Thank You | Team name, contact, repo link |

---

## Timing

**Total: 7–10 minutes** — then judges ask questions.

- Problem & Solution: 3–4 min
- Demo: 2–3 min
- Why Blockchain / Avalanche: 1 min
- Impact & Next Steps: 1 min

Practice until your team can do it in under 10 minutes.

---

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

❌ Reading from slides — show that you know what you are talking about.

❌ Explaining blockchain basics — assume judges know what blockchain is. Focus on your project.

❌ Big vague claims — "We will bank the unbanked in every African country" needs a real plan. Small honest projects beat huge empty ones.

❌ Not showing the demo until the end — show it early. It is the most interesting part.

❌ One person doing all the talking — let each team member speak about their part.

❌ Overly complicated slides — keep text short, use visuals and screenshots.

---

## What Judges Care About Most

1. **Is the problem real?** — Can you explain it clearly?
2. **Does the demo work?** — Show it. Even a simple demo counts.
3. **Do you understand your own project?** — Not just the code, but why it matters.
4. **Is blockchain needed here?** — Is the fit natural or forced?
5. **Can this go further?** — Realistic next steps, not fantasy.

---

## Quick Checklist Before You Present

- [ ] Demo works on the presentation laptop.
- [ ] Wallets / accounts are ready and logged in.
- [ ] Test data is loaded (not empty screens).
- [ ] Slides are short — no walls of text.
- [ ] Team knows who says what.
- [ ] Total time under 10 minutes.
- [ ] GitHub repo has a README with your project info.

---

Good luck. Build small. Demo clearly. Tell a real story. 🚀
